I. TF-IDF Analysis

First analyze words of highest TF-IDF as they are considered "the most important words" in the fairy tale word sets. I will use a babble graph to show the most common words in the graph. The size of the babbles represent the relative importance of the words. The color heat of the words represent the word length, as longer words are "hotter". On the right of each babble graph is the interpretation of the data.

English TD-IDF Analysis

Looking at the high TF-IDF words in the English fairy tales, we can see that most of the words are of the length 3 or 4, which correspond to the color "light blue" and "grey". This is largely due to the audiences of the English fairy tales are kids. Noticably we can see that the average word length (thus average heat of the color of circles) here is relatively low compared to fairy tales from Russia or India. This is probably due to that the other stories are usually translated and simople words in foreign languages may or may be be easily expressed in English during translation.

One thing noticeable is that English fairy tales are obsessed with using the name "Jack", and they usually prefer "cat" then "dog" in their stories.

Russian TD-IDF Analysis

Looking at the Russian TF-IDF words in the Russian fairy tales, we can clearly see some regional words such as "tsarevich" which means the first prince of the kingdom. In this respect, it is similar to English fairy tales since "prince" is a word of high frequency in those fairy tales.

In contrast to typical English fairy tales. Russian fairy tales don't have as many animals as English fairy tales do. Instead, based on the words of high importance, the fairy tales are more related to human beings, and are usually more so to the royal members. For examples, the word king, prince, princess, and fool are very common. This could largely indicate the ancient word recorded fairy tales themes in Russia are largely distributed among royal settings.

Indian TD-IDF Analysis

We do see some Indian only word such as "faja", or "fakir" which are names of Muslim religious. However, the Indian fairy tales share the same theme as the English and Russian fairy tales. Its jokes are mostly centered towards royal members such as kings and princes, and towards animals such as horses and dogs. It may occur that all over the world, the fairy tales are likely to include animals and royal animals due to those content's benign nature.

Different from English and Russian fairy tales, the word "son" has a high importance in the Indian fairy tales. This may indicate the Asian background of the Indian fairy tales, where in ancient Asia, people are largely concerned about family heritage and therefore the fairy tales mention about father and son relationships.

Look at the graph for drives for the fairy tales. We can easily discover that Indian Fairy tales largely focus on drives, affiliation, achieve, power, and risk.
