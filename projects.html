1. Research on bank systemic risk

Research objective:

Model how systemic risks transmit through bank systems

Research type:

Independent research paid with credit, organized by Professor Zachary Feinstein in WashU, worked independently and reported to professor once a week

What I did:

  • Read research papers about bank default risk. I used their mathematical methods to solve non-linear systems and calculated clearing vectors.
  • Set up the model in MATLAB using object-oriented programming, and gradually added features to the model, such as extension of debts and the settlement of transactions.
  • Optimized the run-time and further expanded the model so it simulated bank behaviors over time and can thus use time series to track financial system evolvement.
  • Created and then implemented the idea where I applied the model to standard simulated bank systems. I generated cases where banks form unions, set up branches, or establish stratified structures where central banks ensure economic stability of regional financial networks. Then, I simulated bank defaults and systematic economic shocks to test the financial sustainability.
